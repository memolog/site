The defining aspect of flex layout is the ability to make the flex items ‚Äúflex‚Äù, altering their width/height to fill the available space in the main dimension. This is done with the flex property. A flex container distributes free space to its items (proportional to their flex grow factor) to fill the container, or shrinks them (proportional to their flex shrink factor) to prevent overflow.

A flex item is fully inflexible if both its flex-grow and flex-shrink values are zero, and flexible otherwise.
